Harbin, China — Multi-Phase Food Processing Program

CSC supported McCain Foods at its Harbin campus for more than eight years (2007 to 2015) with rolling upgrades and expansions. The program delivered FM (Factory Mutual), UL (Underwriters Laboratories), and FDA (Food and Drug Administration) qualified systems across process production, temperature control, and site infrastructure.

Photos below are a small representative slice from a much larger body of work.

Project Overview

CSC served as Owner’s Representative and program partner through multiple phases, coordinating contractors and certifying authorities, aligning design and construction with food safety and insurance requirements, and keeping delivery on track through challenging weather and site conditions.

Program Value and Scale

  • $7.6M Food Processing Plant upgrade.
  • $8.1M Wastewater lagoon for the food processing plant.
  • $4.0M FM, UL, and FDA qualified process production line.
  • $6.7M FM, UL, and FDA qualified temperature-controlled facility.
  • $3.0M FM, UL, and FDA qualified food process line.
  • Eight-year engagement with phased turnovers and continuous improvements.

Key Deliverables

  • Process line expansions and upgrades with sanitary detailing and QA/QC oversight.
  • Temperature-controlled rooms and refrigeration systems, cooling tower work, and electrical room tie-ins.
  • Wastewater lagoon construction and associated site civil improvements.
  • Potato storage complexes (Bins 9 through 12) with structure, foundations, and envelopes.

Execution

  • Owner’s Representative services, schedule and cost control, field decision support.
  • FM, UL, and FDA qualification paths integrated into design and turnover.
  • Contractor coordination, site safety, and inspection management through heavy rain seasons.
